Pelé

The King of Football

Pelé's Career Timeline:

  • 1940 - Pelé is born as Edson Arantes do Nascimento in Três Corações, Brazil.
  • 1956 - In September, at age 15, Pelé joins Santos FC, beginning his professional football career.
  • 1957 - Makes his debut for the Brazilian national team, showcasing his talent on an international stage.
  • 1958 - Plays in his first FIFA World Cup in Sweden. On June 29, Brazil wins the World Cup, with Pelé scoring twice in the final, marking a historic achievement for the young star.
  • 1962 - Plays in the FIFA World Cup in Chile. On June 17, Brazil wins their second consecutive World Cup, further solidifying Pelé's status as a football legend.
  • 1966 - Participates in the FIFA World Cup in England. Despite high expectations, Brazil is eliminated in the group stage, facing strong competition.
  • 1970 - Plays in the FIFA World Cup in Mexico. On June 21, Brazil wins the World Cup, marking Pelé’s third World Cup victory. Pelé scores in the final, leading his team to another historic triumph.
  • 1974 - Pelé retires from Santos FC after nearly two decades with the club, leaving behind a legacy of extraordinary achievements and countless goals.
  • 1975 - Pelé comes out of retirement to play for the New York Cosmos in the North American Soccer League (NASL), helping to popularize soccer in the United States.
  • 1977 - Plays his final professional match in an exhibition game between the New York Cosmos and Santos FC, bringing an end to his illustrious playing caree
  • 1980-2000s - Serves as a global ambassador for soccer, working with various organizations including the United Nations and UNESCO to promote peace, education, and the sport of football.
  • 1999 - Pelé is named "Athlete of the Century" by the International Olympic Committee, recognizing his unparalleled contributions to sports and his status as one of the greatest athletes of all time.
  • 2014 - Pelé is honored at the FIFA World Cup in Brazil as part of the opening ceremonies, celebrating his enduring legacy in the world of football.
  • 2022 - Pelé continues to be celebrated worldwide for his contributions to soccer and his humanitarian efforts, remaining a beloved and iconic figure in the sport
  • 2023 - On December 29, Pelé passes away at the age of 82. The world mourns the loss of a football legend, reflecting on his incredible career and the indelible impact he made on the sport and beyond.
